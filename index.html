Top highlights

Five headline wins · one per department
Coastal restoration · MBE 01

Biscayne Bay oyster restoration triples filtration, in Nature Ecology & Evolution

Prof. Marcus Vandermeer and the Coastal Restoration Lab report that three years of large-scale oyster planting across 12 Biscayne Bay sites have tripled local filtration capacity and triggered seagrass recovery — the first at-scale demonstration in U.S. waters.

Vandermeer et al., Nat. Ecol. Evol. (2026)
Operational hurricane ML · ATM 02

SHIPS-ML+ calls Hurricane Erika's rapid intensification 36 hours out

Bhatt & Morán's operational ML model — deployed at NHC on 1 July — flagged Hurricane Erika's Cat-2 → Cat-4 jump 36 hours in advance. Prof. Naveed Karim's new boundary-layer instrumentation rode the NOAA P-3 through the eyewall.

First operational success · NHC + AOML
Submarine geohazards · MGS 03

First high-resolution submarine landslide atlas for the SE US shelf

Prof. Tomas Larsen's team publishes a comprehensive bathymetric and seismic atlas of submarine landslide hazards along the southeastern US continental margin — with direct implications for tsunami-risk modeling from Cape Hatteras to the Keys.

Larsen et al., Geology (2026)
Ocean climate · OCE 04

22-year Argo record reveals accelerated South Atlantic warming, in Science

Prof. Helena Aguilar and PhD student Pierre Toussaint synthesize two decades of Argo float and satellite data: the South Atlantic is warming at roughly twice the global ocean rate — a leading indicator for AMOC weakening.

Aguilar & Toussaint, Science (2026)
Climate policy · EVR 05

Florida's first statewide climate adaptation framework signed into law

Prof. Ben Yamamoto and the Coastal Resilience Group draft the technical foundation for Florida's first statewide climate-adaptation framework — adopted unanimously by the state legislature on 12 August 2026.

Drafted with the FL Climate Resilience Office

Funding mix · This month

A balanced cross-department month

August spreads new dollars across all five departments — the most balanced funding month since the new strategic plan.
