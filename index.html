Top highlights

Five headline wins · one per department
Coral resilience · MBE 01

Heat-tolerant coral genotypes published in Nature

Delgado's Coral Resilience Lab identifies three Caribbean genotypes that survived the 2023–24 bleaching with intact symbiont function — 1,200 fragments now outplanted at three Keys reef sites.

Delgado et al., Nature 631, 188–194 (2026)
Hurricane forecasting · ATM 02

ML rapid-intensification model goes operational at NHC

Bhatt & Morán's “SHIPS-ML+” cuts 24-hr RI false alarms by 31% in 2024–25 hindcasts. Now in operational test at the National Hurricane Center for the 2026 season.

NOAA JTTI · with NHC & AOML
NSF CAREER · MGS 03

NSF CAREER award for slow-slip imaging in Cascadia

Okonkwo's 5-year, $785K award funds high-resolution imaging of slow-slip events along the Cascadia margin and a new “Listening to the Earth” module for K-12 classrooms.

Solid Earth · NSF EAR-2614402
Ocean transport · OCE 04

First 7-year cabled-array Florida Current record

Karpov, Olsson & the FC-Array team publish the longest continuous Florida Current transport record to date — revealing significant variability linked to AMOC.

Karpov et al., L&O (2026)
Community resilience · EVR 05

South Florida's largest community flood-sensor network goes live

Reyes-Tobias & Cortez deploy 28 low-cost sensors with neighborhood partners across Little Haiti, Liberty City, and Shorecrest — co-designed with frontline residents.

NOAA Sea Grant · Env. Sci. & Policy (2026)
